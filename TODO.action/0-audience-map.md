# Audience map — who uses the G 18 Registry and what they need

## Audiences

### 1. TC 1 / Vocabularies (harmonizers)
**Role:** Owns G 18 as a vocabulary. Harmonizes term definitions across
all OIML publications. Decides which definitions merge, which stay
separate, and what the canonical 202X wording should be.

**Primary questions:**
- Which terms have conflicting definitions across publications?
- Which terms cite outdated VIM/VIML editions?
- Which IDs are assigned to multiple concepts?
- Which terms are clean and can be standardized as-is?

**Pages:** Actions, Defn Conflicts, ID Conflicts, Editions

---

### 2. TC/SC Secretaries (managers)
**Role:** Oversees the OIML Recommendations produced by their TC/SC.
Needs to know if any of their publications' terms need updating to
align with G 18:202X.

**Primary questions:**
- Which of MY publications have terms that cite outdated VIM/VIML?
- Which of MY publications have terms with divergent definitions?
- Which of MY publications are clean (no action needed)?
- What specific changes should my project teams make?

**Pages:** TC/SC detail page (per-committee dashboard)

---

### 3. Recommendation Project Teams (authors)
**Role:** Authors of specific OIML Recommendations (R 76-1, D 31, etc.).
Visit the registry to see how their publication's terms compare to the
harmonized G 18 baseline.

**Primary questions:**
- Which terms in MY publication should adopt the VIM/VIML definition?
- Which terms should upgrade to the latest VIM/VIML edition?
- Which terms are unique to MY publication (OIML-original, no VIM ref)?
- Which terms need harmonization with other publications?

**Pages:** Publication detail page (per-publication action list)

---

### 4. General public / metrologists (readers)
**Role:** Looking up a term's definition, usage, or symbol.

**Primary questions:**
- What does term X mean in OIML context?
- What's the authoritative VIM/VIML definition?
- Which publications cite this term?

**Pages:** Terms list, per-term page
